The Norfolk State Unversity Digital Forensics Institute

IA-REDI » Digital Forensics Institute

Overview

Norfolk State University Digital Forensics institute was started with funds from the DoD IASP 2010 Cybersecurity Capacity building grant. These modest funds provided Digital Forensics hardware and software to the tune of $40,000 and development of Digital Forensic courses and course modules.

Currently the center is managed by members of the IA-REDI committee, however it expected in the future that the Digital Forensics institute will function as an autonomous sub center of IA-REDI.

  • Digital Network Forensics
  • Advanced Topics in Digital Forensics

 

Current Research

Research is currently being performed on Steganography, Steganalysis, Wireless Digital Forensics and Digital Forensics in the Cloud

 

 

Future Plans

The DoD Cyber Crime Center (DC3) is proposing starting in 2012 a National Centers of Digital Forensics Academic Excellence Program http://www.dc3.mil/cdfae/ Applications will commence in the latter part of 2012 and the Norfolk State University Digital Foorensics institute will be submitting an application to be designated as a National Center of Digital Forensics Academic Excellence. Our progress based on the draft criteria can be charted at this site.
