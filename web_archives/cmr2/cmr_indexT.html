Center for Materials Research


The Center provides cutting edge research opportunities in well-equipped research facilities using state of the art instrumentation for our graduate and undergraduate science majors. Norfolk State University's Center for Materials Research was established to enhance the materials research capabilities at the university, to encourage faculty development of materials research activities, and to increase the number of minority students obtaining baccalaureate and graduate degrees in materials science disciplines.

 

 

Research Activities at the Center for Materials



ELECTRONIC
SPIN RESONANCE (ERS)


Electronic Spin Resonance

ERS research focuses on the investigatin of magnetic properties of inorganic and organic materials for photonics and spin electronics applications.

NANO-STRUCTURED MATERIALS & MULTILAYERED THIN FILMS

NANO-STRUCTURED MATERIALS & MULTILAYERED THIN FILMS

We concentrate on continued growth and new challenges in nanomaterials, engineering, and nanotechnology, both for application development and for basic research. Research on Nanoscale and nanostructured materials with emphisis on synthesis, processing, charaterization, and applications of materials containing true nanosize dimensions or nanostructures that enable novel/enhanced properties.

NOVEL NONLINEAR MATERIALS & LASER SOURCES

Novel Nonlinear Materials & Laser Sources Laboratory

Novel nonlinear materials and laser sources are interest for the use in communications atmospheric sensing and various othe mission tasks. We are developing photonic materials based on scattering and composite (dielectric/metallic) media. The reserch is focused in the development of unique robust in operation random and comosite laser sources and nonlinear optical materials.

COMPUTATIONAL MATERIALS

Computational Materials

Computational materials science is an interdisciplinary subject that implies the synergy of mathematics, computer science, physics and chemistry. Computational research activity in the Center of Materials Research (CMR) includes numberical modeling and diverse properties of materials (solid, organic molecules, and polymers).
