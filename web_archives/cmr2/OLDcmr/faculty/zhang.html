Research Interest

 Current research interests are in the material design & synthesis, and device fabrication for various optoelectronic  and electro-optic applications, including solar cell, EO modulator, etc.

·          Semiconducting polymers for solar cells (In collaboration with Prof. Sam Sun)

Low-bandgap conjugated polymers, including polythienylenevinylene (PTV), sulfone-PTV, for solar energy conversion and IR detection.

Short side-chain polythiophenes for highly efficient solar cells.

Molecular geometry engineering of low-bandgap polymers for better self-assembly in thin films.  Polythiophene derivatives based on 4H-cyclopenta[2,1-b;3,4-b'] dithiophene-4-one (CPDTO) have been designed with desirable side-chain substitution patterns for photovoltaic conversion.

·          Design and synthesis of novel dipolar molecules that can self-assemble into non-centrosymmetric nanocrystals.

Second-order nonlinear optic chromophores are highly dipolar. Intermolecular dipole interactions tend to force chromophores to form antiparallel aggregates which can not be aligned by external poling field. As a result, bulk nonliearity of chromophore-incorparated polymer films is far less than that in the ideal situation in which all chromophores are perfectly aligned. Prof. Zhang has proposed to reduce and hopefully eliminate the antiparallel packing force between dipoles by building a macrocycles around the center of a chomophore (a ring at the waist). The ring will keep a good distance between dipoles and makes antiparallel stacking energetically less favorable than the desirable head-to-tail interaction. With the specially-shaped chromophores, EO activity could be enhanced by one order of magnitude, and driving voltage of EO moducation devices can to reduced to around 200mV.
