Welcome

The Computer Science Graduate Program at Norfolk State University provides a quality computer science graduate education to students, especially those from the underrepresented sector of the population, by sstrenghtening analytic skills, offering valuable research experiences, and promoting professional development in computer scinece. The Masters of Science (M.S) degree requires 30 graduate credit hours of course work including a thesis (6 credits), or 33 graduate credit hours of course work including a project (3 credits). The program has four areas of study:

  • Computer Science (General Option)
  • Emphasis in Information Assurance
  • Emphasis in Computational Science & Engineering
  • Emphasis in Communications Networks
